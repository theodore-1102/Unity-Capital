# Unity Capital

Please refer to Unity Capital_SecondariesAI_vDemo

This project develops a private equity secondary market intelligence platform designed to improve how private equity (PE) firms evaluate and execute liquidity transactions across their portfolios. The platform aggregates transaction data, comparable deals, and market intelligence into a single interface that enables PE firms to assess pricing, buyer demand, and execution pathways more efficiently. It integrates datasets from sources such as S&P Capital IQ, market news feeds, and transaction databases, while providing portfolio tracking, comparable transaction analysis, and AI-driven insights through an integrated interface. 

The core objective of the platform is to address structural inefficiencies in the private equity secondary market, where pricing transparency and real-time intelligence remain limited. Today, PE firms seeking liquidity for fund interests or assets typically rely heavily on advisors and relationship networks to gauge buyer appetite and indicative pricing. This results in fragmented information flows and delayed decision-making. By centralising transaction data and comparable deals into a structured database, the platform allows users to quickly identify relevant benchmarks and estimate realistic pricing ranges before formally engaging advisors.

The platform also supports portfolio monitoring and deal screening. PE firms can upload their assets, analyse historical transactions involving similar funds or companies, and assess likely discounts to net asset value (NAV). This capability allows investment teams to identify which assets are most likely to clear the market and which may require alternative structures such as continuation vehicles or staged liquidity solutions.

It also introduces a workflow framework aligned with the typical secondary transaction process, covering stages from liquidity evaluation and advisor selection to market outreach and transaction execution. By mapping comparable transactions and buyer behaviour at each stage, the system provides clearer visibility into expected timelines, pricing dispersion, and structural considerations.

The project solves several critical industry pain points. First, it reduces information asymmetry by providing transparent pricing benchmarks and comparable transactions. Second, it lowers dependency on intermediaries, enabling PE firms to enter advisor discussions with stronger market intelligence. Third, it improves execution efficiency by reducing the trial-and-error nature of portfolio structuring and pricing negotiations. Finally, by consolidating market signals and transaction data, the platform helps firms minimise failed placements, delayed closings, and unnecessary discounts on secondary transactions.
