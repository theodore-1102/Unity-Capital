Secondary Portfolio Structure Guide

Practitioner reference for LP-led and GP-led secondary transactions.

Last updated: Mar 2026
Classification: Internal

1. Market Overview

The PE secondary market has matured into a $130B+ annual market (2025E), bifurcated into LP-led (LPs selling fund interests) and GP-led (GPs restructuring portfolios into new vehicles).

DimensionLP-LedGP-Led
Initiating PartySelling LPGP / Manager
Typical Discount5-30% to NAV0-10% (often par for quality)
Timeline6-24 weeks12-36 weeks
Market Share (2025E)~48%~52%

2. LP-Led Secondaries

2.1 Strip Sale vs. Mosaic Sale

Strip Sale
  • Definition: Entire portfolio offered as a single package
  • Discount: Wider (2-5% incremental) - buyer absorbs unwanted positions
  • Transactable NAV: 100% - full liquidity
  • Best For: Regulatory capital, fund wind-down
Mosaic / Cherry-Pick
  • Definition: Each fund interest bid individually
  • Discount: Tighter on premium (0-5%) but 60-85% NAV transacts
  • Residual Risk: Tail stub unsold or deeply discounted
  • Best For: High-quality portfolios seeking best blended price
Key Insight: A $500M strip sale at 88-92% generates full liquidity. Via mosaic, top $350M achieves 95-100% but $150M tail trades at 70-80% - lower blended realisation.

2.2 GP-for-Hire Solutions

For $1B+ portfolios across 20-40 funds, a specialist GP manages runoff over 2-5 years rather than outright sale. Fee: 25-75bps mgmt + 5-10% carry. Best when immediate sale pricing is unattractive or portfolio contains illiquid interests.

2.3 Impact of Deferrals

Deferral Pricing Impact

Each 10% deferred consideration = 50-100bps additional effective discount to buyer. Sellers should negotiate SOFR + 200-400bps interest accrual on deferred amounts or price floor mechanisms.

2.4 Preferred Equity

Buyer provides preferred tranche (30-50% NAV) earning 8-12% before LP participates. If portfolio expected to generate 1.5x+ MOIC, preferred may beat outright sale at 15-20% discount. Key: subordination mechanics, distribution waterfall, control rights, capital call treatment.

3. GP-Led Secondaries

3.1 Single-Asset CVs

Dominant GP-led structure (~50% of volume). GP transfers one high-performing company into new vehicle.

ElementTypical Terms
Vehicle Term3-5 years (+2x1yr ext.)
GP Economics1.0-1.5% mgmt; 10-15% carry above 8% pref
ValuationIndependent fairness opinion required
RolloverExisting LPs can roll 100% at CV price

3.2 Multi-Asset CVs

3-8 companies into one vehicle. Pricing at 90-97% due to adverse selection risk and blending. Best practice: full data rooms, independent QoE, management presentations per asset.

3.3 Tender Offers & Stapled Secondaries

Tender: GP facilitates buyer offer to LPs. Fund continues unchanged. Stapled: secondary purchase + primary fund commitment (e.g., $200M secondary + $100M Fund IX). Effective discount 2-4% tighter than standalone.

3.4 NAV Financing vs. GP-Led

NAV loans create fund-level leverage and margin call exposure with no valuation event. GP-led secondaries provide arm's-length pricing and elective LP liquidity without additional leverage.

Portfolio Construction

Target 50-60% LP-led (1.4-1.7x MOIC) and 40-50% GP-led (1.5-2.0x+ MOIC). Firms bridging fund-level and company-level underwriting will generate the best risk-adjusted returns in the 2025-2030 vintage.
